4.1 Data Modality

In AI, modality refers to the type of sensory information a model processes. Modern AI systems are moving from unimodal (one type) to multimodal (multiple types) capabilities.

Types of Data Modality

  • Text: Natural language data used in LLMs (GPT, BERT).
  • Image: Visual data used in Computer Vision (Convolutional Neural Networks).
  • Audio: Sound waves processed for Speech-to-Text and Music generation.
  • Sensor: Structured numerical data from IoT devices, GPS, or medical equipment.

4.2 Data Quality & Bias

The quality of your data directly determines the quality of your AI. "Garbage in, garbage out" is the golden rule of machine learning.

Key Data Challenges

  • Data Bias: When training data is not representative of the real population, leading to unfair predictions.
  • Missing Values: Gaps in data that can skew statistical significance.
  • Dirty Data: Incorrect, duplicate, or irrelevant entries that confuse the model.

Handling Bias

Mitigating bias requires a multi-step approach: diverse data sourcing, algorithmic fairness checks, and continuous monitoring of model outputs across different demographic groups.
