1.1 Statistical Decision Logic

Mean, Variance & the Boutique Pulse

The Strategic Logic

A manager's primary role is to maintain the "Mean" (Average) and minimize "Variance" (Risk). Mastering statistical functions turns raw sales data into actionable intelligence.

Central Tendency

Using SUM, AVERAGE, and MEDIAN to identify the "Boutique Pulse" — the heartbeat of your sales channel.

  • SUM: Total revenue across a period; essential for top-line performance reporting.
  • AVERAGE: The arithmetic mean; the primary benchmark for forecasting targets.
  • MEDIAN: The middle value; more resistant to extreme outliers than AVERAGE. Used to understand typical customer spend.

The Volatility Check

Utilizing STDEV.P to measure sales consistency — the single most important metric for inventory planning.

  • High Std Dev: Indicates unpredictable footfall.
    → Requires higher "Safety Stock" levels to prevent stockouts.
  • Low Std Dev: Indicates stable demand.
    → Allows for "Just-in-Time" inventory, reducing holding cost and improving cash flow.

Outlier Detection

Using MIN and MAX to identify "Hero Products" versus "Dead Stock" that is consuming liquid capital.
