6. Slice & Dice

Filtering While Calculating

The Strategic Logic

Aggregated data can lie. A total revenue figure doesn't show you the performance of specific products in specific regions. Professionals use conditional aggregation to find the underlying truth behind the numbers.

1. The "IFS" Family: Multi-Criteria Power

Moving beyond basic SUMIF to complex queries (SUMIFS, COUNTIFS) allows for deep-dive analysis.

  • Business Query: "What is the total revenue of Linen fabric in the Mumbai branch during Q1?"
  • The Result: A single formula that looks through thousands of rows and returns the exact segment value without manual filtering.

2. AVERAGEIFS: Strategic Benchmarking

Comparing performance across subsets of data to detect leakage or outlier performance.

  • Managerial Audit: Compare the Average Discount given by Store Manager A vs. Store Manager B. If A averages 15% and B averages 5%, you've detected potential profit leakage.
