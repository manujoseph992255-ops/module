3.1 AI Risks and Ethics

As AI systems become more powerful, the risks they pose grow in complexity. Ethical development is not an option; it is a necessity for trust and safety.

Common AI Risks

  • Bias: Models inheriting human prejudices from training data.
  • Privacy: Unauthorized use of sensitive personal information.
  • Misinformation: AI-generated "deepfakes" and false content.
  • Job Displacement: Automation replacing human roles without a transition plan.

3.2 Safeguarding AI Systems

Security in AI involves protecting the model, the data, and the deployment environment from malicious actors.

Security Best Practices

  • Adversarial Robustness: Training models to resist manipulated inputs.
  • Data Sanctity: Ensuring training data has not been "poisoned" by attackers.
  • Model Encryption: Protecting the proprietary weights and logic of the AI.
