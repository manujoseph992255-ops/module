1.1 Introduction to AI

Understanding the Essence of Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the branch of computer science that deals with the creation of intelligent agents, which are systems that can reason, learn, and act autonomously.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think and learn like humans. These machines can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation.

Features of Artificial Intelligence

  • Automation: Performing tasks without human intervention.
  • Machine Learning: Ability of machines to learn from data and improve over time.
  • Logic and Reasoning: Solving complex problems using structured logic.
  • Decision Making: Analyzing information to choose the best course of action.

Goals of Artificial Intelligence

The primary goals of AI include:

  • To Create Expert Systems: Systems that exhibit intelligent behavior, learn, demonstrate, explain, and advise its users.
  • To Implement Human Intelligence in Machines: Creating systems that understand, think, learn, and behave like humans.

1.2 Real-World Applications

AI is no longer a futuristic concept—it is embedded in our daily lives through multiple applications:

  • Healthcare: AI assists in diagnosing diseases, personalized treatment, and robotic surgery.
  • Finance: Used for fraud detection, algorithmic trading, and personalized banking recommendations.
  • Transportation: Powers self-driving cars, route optimization, and traffic management systems.
  • E-commerce: Recommendation engines (Amazon/Netflix), dynamic pricing, and inventory management.

As we progress through this course, we will explore how these systems are built, the data they require, and the ethical considerations that guide their development.
